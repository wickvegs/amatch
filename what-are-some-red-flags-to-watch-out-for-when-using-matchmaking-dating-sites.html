What are some red flags to watch out for when using matchmaking dating sites?

Matchmaking sites are great for making connections with people that you might not otherwise meet in real life. They allow you to filter for certain attributes like age, location, interests, etc. to find people who are more compatible with your dating style. However, with so many people joining these sites every day, it can be tough to find genuine potential partners. If you aren’t careful, you could end up getting scammed or talking to someone who isn’t interested in you whatsoever. In order to avoid falling into any of these situations, we recommend using these red flags to spot dishonest users on dating sites.

Too Good to Be True

If someone seems too good to be true, they probably are. If someone is too generous with their praise, too eager to meet you, or too eager to send you a gift, they’re probably not genuine. If a person is too eager to meet you or wants to talk on the phone with you almost immediately, they might not have any genuine intentions. If someone is only interested in you if you have a lot of money or if they can get something out of you, they’re probably not interested in a relationship.

Bias against certain groups

If a dating site is actively discriminating against a certain group, that’s a big red flag. If a site is only allowing users to be of a certain age or to have a certain type of job, they’re discriminating against users who don’t fit those criteria. If a site is actively disallowing users based on who they are, that’s a huge red flag. If a site is only allowing users to join if they have a certain level of income, they’re discriminating against people who don’t have a lot of money.

Too many users who disappear
